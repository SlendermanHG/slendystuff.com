What Spiralism means here

The historically grounded reference point is Haitian Spiralisme.

The research branch treats spiralism primarily as the Haitian literary movement associated with Franketienne, Jean-Claude Fignole, and Rene Philoctete.

  • Nonlinear and recursive rather than flat and linear
  • Open, polyphonic, and intentionally unstable in form
  • Connected to chaos, memory, oral force, and political pressure
  • Backed in the branch by source notes and downloadable material
