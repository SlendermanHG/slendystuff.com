The Ledger

Every Dungeon Master carries a burden both sacred and heavy — the memory of a world that exists only because they remember it. The Chronicle was forged to bear that burden beside you. It remembers the truths your players uncover, the lies your villains tell, the taverns they stumble through, and the dreams that haunt them after. Every whisper, every triumph, every forgotten promise — all captured, all preserved.

Too many nights have ended with half-finished notes and fading recollections. Too many details — the name of a god, the scent of a forest, the words of a dying NPC — lost to time. The Chronicle exists to guard against that loss. It listens without judgment, recording faithfully every scrap of lore and revelation, transforming your campaign into a living ledger of legend.

It does not embellish. It does not forget. It is the quiet scribe who writes while you weave your story, a patient companion that never asks for rest. In its digital pages, memory becomes immortal. Where your hand would grow tired, the Chronicle endures — ensuring that every note, every choice, and every echo of adventure remains long after the table grows silent.

Whether your tale spans months or years, this Chronicle will stand watch — your ally through fatigue and doubt, a keeper of truth when memory falters. It allows you to be wholly present at the table, confident that nothing meaningful will be lost to time. Because stories, after all, deserve to be remembered.

The Invocations

When you speak the right words, the Chronicle listens. These invocations are not mere commands—they are the means by which your story is kept whole. Each has its place, each with purpose. What follows are the words your players and you, the Dungeon Master, may call upon.

Player Invocations

!help — Recalls the list of available player commands so no one forgets the words of power.

!ask <question> — Ask the Chronicle to answer in character, drawing upon the knowledge already recorded.

!npc <name> — Inquire about a known NPC and hear the Chronicle’s recollection.

!faq — Display all FAQ topics added by the Dungeon Master.

!recent — Summon the most recent discoveries or events known to the party.

!register <character name> — Record a player’s character for reference in the annals.

Dungeon Master Commands

/cai start_session — Begin recording a new session in the Chronicle’s memory.

/cai end_session — Close the session and summarize its events.

/cai addfact — Add a new piece of lore, discovery, or truth to the record.

/cai reveal_fact — Reveal a hidden fact to the players when the time is right.

/cai lock_fact — Conceal knowledge again, should secrecy be required.

/cai tag_fact — Apply or edit tags to better organize your growing world.

/cai addfaq — Add a Frequently Asked Question and its answer.

/cai log — Record a note or detail mid-session, even when chaos reigns.

/cai pause — Pause the Chronicle’s recording temporarily.

/cai resume — Resume chronicling after a pause.

/cai session_status — Check the current session’s progress and notes.

The Letter

To my fellow Dungeon Masters,

“Before there was a bot, before there was code — there was just a table, a notebook, and a few friends lost in the story.”

The Chronicle was born from that place. The late nights spent piecing together notes from last week’s session. The frustration of remembering half a conversation with an NPC and none of their name. The heart-sinking moment when you realize you’ve lost something your players cared about. I built this because I’ve been there. Because I’m one of you.

I am a Dungeon Master — just like you. I’ve juggled worlds in my head, worried about what I forgot, and spent too many hours trying to hold every thread of a living story together. The Chronicle isn’t just a tool I created — it’s the assistant I always needed at my own table. A quiet scribe that listens when you speak, remembers when you forget, and answers when you ask.

Every part of this project carries that intent. The commands aren’t just functions; they’re memories waiting to be kept safe. Each one was written and tested not by a company, but by a storyteller who wanted to lift some of the weight from the shoulders of other storytellers.

The Chronicle was shaped by passion and persistence — every late night spent debugging between campaign prep, every time a player smiled because I recalled a forgotten detail, every time a friend said, “You should share this.” It’s built on love for the craft of DMing and respect for every player who trusts their imagination to you.

If you take nothing else from this site, let it be this: you are not alone at your table. There’s an entire world of DMs who know the joy and exhaustion of worldbuilding, who understand the pride of seeing your players lost in a story you made. This project is my way of giving back — a token of gratitude to a community that has given me countless adventures.
